The text states that, “A key belief on Hinduism is the concept of reincarnation, or the rebirth of a soul.”

When does this process end?

This process ends when a new reincarnated being lives the perfect life.

By the early 1200’s, the geographic area that was under Muslim rule was northern India (“...in the established Delhi sultanate”).

n light of Muslim rule, how were Hindu subjects still able to practice Hinduism?

Hindu subjects were still able to practice Hinduism because Muslim rulers practiced “tolerance” and, Hinduism was still able to be India’s main religion

Islam became an established religion in India because of both trade with Africa and the Turkish invasions in Central Asia. Although Hinduism “remained India’s main religion”, Islam was starting to spread because Indians were either forced to become Muslim or decided to become Muslim because they didn’t want to have to pay, “a special”, tax.

The Khmer Empire

Main Idea: Rice agriculture helped the Khmer dominate Southeast Asia for centuries

Indian and Chinese Influences

Where did the Khmer people migrate from?

The Khmer people migrated from China.

What was Chenla?

Chenla were small city states that were founded by 500 (AD).

What powerful trading kingdom was to their south?

Funan was the powerful trending kingdom that was to the south of Chenla.

What Indian influences did Funan introduce to Cambodia?

The Indian influences that were introduced to Cambodia from Funan were: centralized government, the sanskrit language, irrigation, and the religion of Hindu.

What did the Chenla kingdoms do in 802AD?

The text states that, “In 802, they formally united under the ‘universal ruler’ Jayavarma II. This was the beginning of the Khmer Empire, which dominated Southeast Asia until 1431.”

The Angkor Era

Where did the Khmer establish their capital city?

Angkor was where the Khmer established their capital city which actually means “city”.

What are Khmer religious beliefs a mixture of?

The text states that, “Khmer religious beliefs were a complex mixture of Hinduism, Buddhism and native religions.”

The large bureaucracy that governed the Khmer Empire included what, according to the text?

According to the text, “A large central bureaucracy governed the Khmer Empire, which included vassal states. These states paid tribute, which, along with trade, contributed to the empire’s economy.”

How did rice prosperity lead to prosperity and power for the Khmer Empire?

Because the Khmer were skilled at rice farming and their regions were great for cultivating, they were able to grow lots of rice. However, they were only able to do this because they “combined immense storage tanks with canals, dikes, and dams.” This way, they were able to produce 3-4 rice harvests a year! And, “This intensive rice cultivation was the foundation of Khmer prosperity, stability, and power, which expanded across Southeast Asia.”

What two factors led to the downfall of the Khmer Empire?

The two factors that led to the downfall of the Khmer Empire were wars and “pouring resources into religious monuments”. Eventually, the capital became abandoned.
